\hmsubsection{Problem Statement}{OAH}{}

How can a four-degree-of-freedom arm with a fifth motor for the gripper, guided
by an OAK-D~S2 RGB camera mounted on the wrist and positioned above the workspace
when the arm is in the scan pose and by a classical computer vision pipeline,
reliably detect, classify, and sort red and blue spherical objects in real time
under varying indoor lighting conditions, without requiring manual intervention
between sorting cycles?
